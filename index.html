Who We Are

About NESAAU

The Nigeria Economics Students Association, Adeleke University Chapter (NESAAU) is the official departmental body representing all Economics students at Adeleke University, Ede, Osun State, Nigeria.

We bridge the gap between theory and practice, organising seminars, providing academic resources, and fostering leadership that prepares members for life beyond the lecture hall.

Our Mission

To promote academic excellence, professional development, and student welfare.

Our Vision

To be the most impactful departmental association in Adeleke University.

NESAAU Constitution — Preview

NESAAU CONSTITUTION

PREAMBLE

We, the students of the Department of Economics, Adeleke University, solemnly resolve to strengthen unity and commitment to promoting academic excellence, mutual growth, and general welfare[cite: 9]. We establish this Constitution for effective governance and progress, recognizing the importance of collaboration and educational resources within and beyond our institution[cite: 10].

ARTICLE 1 — NAME & IDENTITY

This body shall be known and addressed as the Nigerian Economics Students' Association Adeleke University (NESAAU)[cite: 31, 34]. This Constitution is supreme, and its provisions are binding on all members, subject to the regulations of Adeleke University and the Federal Republic of Nigeria[cite: 36]. Members of this Association are officially addressed as "NESITES"[cite: 39].

ARTICLE 2 — MEMBERSHIP

Membership is composed of all students in the Department of Economics who are duly registered through the payment of session dues[cite: 189]. Registered members have the right to use Association facilities, wear official merchandise, vote and be voted for in approved elections, and obtain a copy of this Constitution[cite: 191, 192, 194].

ARTICLE 3 — DUES & FINANCES

An agreed annual sum of ₦2,000 must be paid by registered chapters to the Treasurer for the efficient running of the Association, while the executive body pays ₦2,500[cite: 218]. Dues amounts are approved by the Executive Council in consultation with the Senate[cite: 197]. Financial standing is required for members to access academic and social criteria privileges[cite: 226].

ARTICLE 4 — ELECTIONS

Elections are conducted annually by an Electoral Commission composed of student representatives from each level[cite: 219, 221, 237]. Aspirants for President, Vice President, and Secretary General must maintain at least a 3.5 CGPA[cite: 227]. Voting is conducted via a simple open-secret ballot system, and candidates are elected by a simple majority[cite: 229, 231].

ARTICLE 5 — CODE OF CONDUCT

Offences include breaches of the Constitution, fraudulent practices, disclosure of confidential matters, and acting in a manner that brings the Association into disrepute[cite: 241, 242, 243, 244]. Sanctions range from caution and restitution to suspension from office or the Association, depending on the seriousness of the offence[cite: 253].

ARTICLE 6 — AMENDMENTS

This Constitution is subject to amendment upon a resolution passed by a two-thirds (2/3) majority of the Senate House[cite: 273]. Amendments must be reviewed during a formally constituted meeting and must not contradict the fundamental objectives outlined in the preamble[cite: 274, 275]. Approved amendments are disseminated to all members and the executive arm[cite: 276].
